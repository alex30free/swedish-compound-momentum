How Compound Momentum Works

01 · What It Measures

Compound Momentum sums a stock's raw percentage price return over three distinct time windows — 3 months, 6 months, and 12 months. A stock that rises strongly across all three windows achieves a high composite score. No weighting: each window contributes equally.

02 · Why Three Windows?

Different momentum horizons capture different phenomena. The 3-month window captures short-term trend acceleration. The 6-month window filters noise and confirms the trend. The 12-month window ensures structural, sustained outperformance — not a brief spike.

03 · Company Size Column

Market capitalisation in million SEK, converted from Yahoo Finance's USD figure. Enables quick identification of micro-cap vs large-cap stocks. Thin liquidity in very small companies can amplify momentum moves and widen spreads.

04 · Piotroski F-Score

A 0–9 quality score based on 9 binary signals across profitability (ROA, cash flow, margins), leverage (debt ratio, current ratio, dilution), and efficiency (operating margin, return on equity). Higher scores indicate stronger financial health.

Compound Momentum Score = Return(3M) + Return(6M) + Return(12M)
